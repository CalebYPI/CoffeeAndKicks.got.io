ABSENTEEISM, EARLY DISMISSAL AND LATECOMING

Absenteeism

Learners may not absent themselves without a valid reason. Learners who have been absent MUST, an the return to school, provide a medical certificate or letter from their parents informing the school of the reason for their absence.

The school will contact parents if a learner has been absent for more than 3 days without notice from the parents.

If a pupil is absent for an examination or test, the onus is on the parent to Inform the school on that day, before 10am. Please email wittebome.high@small.com or call the office on 021 7611535.

Please make doctor, dental or specialist appointments after school hours so that the academic program not unduly disrupted.

Early dismissal

If circumstances arise when the dismissal time has to change (as specified in point 3. SCHOOL HOURS), parents will be informed by SMS or letter. Please make sure the school has your correct cell phone number and update any changes to keep this channel of communication effective. .No learner may leave the school premises during school hours without written permission from the principal.

Late coming

Learners are expected to be punctual. Please ensure that your child gets to school on time. Latecomers will have to report to detention. Habitual latecomers will have to report to Saturday detentions.

TEXTBOOKS AND STATIONERY

Learners will be issued with textbooks as per subject. However, these textbooks remain the property of the Western Cape Education Department and must be returned, in good condition, at the end of the year. If any of these textbooks are lost or damaged, they will have to be replaced by the learner at his/her own COst or the cost of the book must be paid at the office.

GENERAL CONDUCT OF LEARNERS

encourage all our learners to take pride in our school and to develop a sense of belonging to the Wittebome family- not only at school, also In the community. We expect that the conduct of Wittebome learners will always be a credit to the school, as each learner Is an ambassador for our school. In our endeavour to achieve this, please note the following:

  • All learners must respect the school, all educators and each other at all times.
  • Learners must also be polite and respectful to all visitors to our school.
  • Learners are expected to do their best to keep our school environment clean.
  • Defacing of desks, books, private property, buildings and plants is not allowed.
  • Bad Language, whether written or spoken, will not be tolerated.
  • The school is a smoke-free zone.
  • Learners must develop a sense of pride in their schoolwork. All work must be completed within the specified time and all presentation must be neat.
  • Where there may be differences, learners must resolve them with the assistance of an educator or peer counsellor.
  • No form of intimidation or bullying will be tolerated
  • The school's Code of Conduct outlines in detail how the breach of these general rules will be addressed
  • Wilful damaging, vandalising or neglect of School Property or the property of others, either by writing or by a physical act, is prohibited.
  • Any act of plagiarism, cheating in class or with class work, Informal and formal tests or internal examinations is prohibited Furthermore, copying of and/or borrowing of another teamer's work is also prohibited. Any copying will be dealt with by an assessment committee

SAFETY OF LEARNERS

The school is a place of safety where laws pertaining to public spaces are applicable: No dangerous objects or illegal substances as defined in the School's Act or Safety Regulations may be brought onto and/or used on the school property Dangerous objects include knives, firearms or any object that could harma person. Alcohol is not permitted on School Premises or during any School Activity such as an excursion. The carrying of and/or consuming of illegal chemical substances, alcohol and drugs are prohibited. According to the School's Act the school may test a learner for any substance any time. If a learner is found guilty of being in possession, or found using, or found to be positive for any drug. the learner could face expulsion,

Suspension and expulsion of Learners

Learners who do not adhere to the rules of the school may face suspension, a Governing Body disciplinary hearing or even expulsion. The Western Cape Education Department is adopting a strict stance on all drug lated Instances and expulsion will be recommended.
